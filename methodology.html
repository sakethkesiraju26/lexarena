Methodology

How LexArena is built and why these predictions actually mean something.

Motivation

Traditional legal datasets test models on historical cases, which modern LLMs can memorize. LexArena evaluates on resolved but unseen outcomes—ensuring clean, out-of-sample testing of an AI system's legal reasoning, uncertainty handling, and predictive depth.

Every forecast is made on complaint text only. Models never see the resolution, eliminating data contamination and retrospective rationalization. No model can have memorized the answer from training data.

This matters because legal prediction requires both factual extraction (understanding the allegations) and probabilistic reasoning (estimating likely outcomes based on precedent, severity, and regulatory patterns).

Benchmark Design

LexArena follows a four-stage pipeline that ensures clean separation between input data and ground truth outcomes.

1

Case Ingestion

We scrape SEC Litigation Releases and extract complaint PDFs as they're filed.

2

Synopsis Generation

GPT-4o creates plain-English case summaries from raw complaint text.

3

Blind Prediction

Models receive only the synopsis and must forecast 6 outcome metrics.

4

Ground Truth Extraction

Outcomes are extracted from final SEC release text after case resolution.

Prediction Metrics

Models predict 6 outcome metrics for each SEC enforcement case. These cover both the nature of resolution and the specific penalties imposed.

Metric Description Scoring
Resolution Type Did the case settle (defendant agrees without trial) or go to litigation (court decides)? Exact Match
Disgorgement Amount of ill-gotten gains the defendant must return to harmed investors. ±10% Tolerance
Civil Penalty Fine imposed by the SEC as punishment for securities law violations. ±10% Tolerance
Prejudgment Interest Interest accrued on disgorgement amount from violation date to judgment. ±10% Tolerance
Injunction Court order preventing future violations of securities laws. Exact Match
Officer/Director Bar Ban from serving as an officer or director of a public company. Exact Match

Scoring

We use different scoring methods depending on the metric type to account for the nature of each prediction.

Categorical Metrics

Resolution, injunction, and officer bar use exact match—the model must predict the correct category.

Monetary Metrics

Disgorgement, penalty, and interest use a 10% tolerance band to account for reasonable estimation variance.

Overall Accuracy

Weighted average across all 6 metrics, with categorical and monetary equally weighted.

Pipeline

The complete evaluation flow from raw SEC filings to leaderboard rankings.

SEC Complaint PDF → Text Extraction → Synopsis Generation → LLM Prediction → Ground Truth Comparison → Score & Rank

Data Integrity

No contamination. Every evaluated case is resolved after the complaint was filed—meaning no model could have seen the outcome in training data.

Consistent prompting. All models receive identical prompts and case summaries, ensuring fair comparison across providers.

Reproducible results. The full scoring code, evaluation prompts, and dataset are open source on GitHub.

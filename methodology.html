Building Legal AGI Starts Here

The future of legal AI depends on verified outcome data. We're creating the foundational dataset for models that reason about legal consequences.

Why Outcomes Matter

Today's LLMs can read legal documents. Tomorrow's will make legal decisions.

The path to Legal AGI isn't more training data—it's the right training data. Models need verified ground truth: what was predicted, what actually happened, and why. That feedback loop doesn't exist at scale.

LexArena is building it. We collect SEC enforcement actions with documented outcomes— not just the complaint, but the resolution: monetary penalties, disgorgement, bars, settlements. Every case becomes a training signal.

This isn't just a benchmark. It's the dataset that future legal reasoning models will learn from.

Who Needs Legal Outcome Data

AI-driven legal prediction is transforming industries that depend on accurate risk assessment from unstructured legal documents.

Insurance Underwriting

D&O and E&O pricing depends on predicting regulatory outcomes. Structured outcome data lets insurers train models that actually improve loss ratios—not just parse documents faster.

Litigation Finance

Case funders need probability distributions on outcomes, not summaries. Training on real resolution data creates the edge.

Foundation Model Labs

Legal reasoning is a key capability for AGI. Labs building next-gen models need high-quality, outcome-verified legal datasets for fine-tuning and RLHF.

Legal AI Startups

Building a legal AI product without outcome data is guessing. LexArena provides the ground truth to train and validate your models.

The Dataset

Each case in LexArena contains structured data paired with verified outcomes:

  • Structured complaint data — extracted via Reducto's document AI
  • AI-generated synopsis — case summary from GPT-4o
  • Verified outcomes — monetary penalties, disgorgement, bars, restrictions
  • Model predictions — what GPT-4o and Claude predicted vs. reality
  • Prediction accuracy — scored across 6 outcome metrics

Every resolved case adds signal. Every prediction adds calibration data. This is how you build a model that reasons about legal outcomes, not just retrieves precedent.

How We Build It

LexArena follows a four-stage pipeline that ensures clean separation between input data and ground truth outcomes.

1

Case Ingestion

We scrape SEC Litigation Releases and extract complaint PDFs as they're filed.

2

Synopsis Generation

GPT-4o creates plain-English case summaries from raw complaint text.

3

Blind Prediction

Models receive only the synopsis and must forecast 6 outcome metrics.

4

Ground Truth Extraction

Outcomes are extracted from final SEC release text after case resolution.

Prediction Metrics

Models predict 6 outcome metrics for each SEC enforcement case. These cover both the nature of resolution and the specific penalties imposed.

Metric Description Scoring
Resolution Type Did the case settle or go to litigation? Exact Match
Disgorgement Amount of ill-gotten gains returned to investors. ±10% Tolerance
Civil Penalty Fine imposed by the SEC as punishment. ±10% Tolerance
Prejudgment Interest Interest accrued on disgorgement amount. ±10% Tolerance
Injunction Court order preventing future violations. Exact Match
Officer/Director Bar Ban from serving at public companies. Exact Match

Scoring

Different metric types require different scoring approaches.

Categorical Metrics

Resolution, injunction, and officer bar use exact match.

Monetary Metrics

Disgorgement, penalty, and interest use 10% tolerance bands.

Overall Accuracy

Weighted average across all 6 metrics.

The Vision

Legal AGI will need to predict outcomes, not just summarize documents. It will need to weigh evidence, assess regulatory risk, and reason about consequences. That capability requires training on verified outcomes at scale.

LexArena is the beginning. We're starting with SEC enforcement—500+ cases with complete outcome resolution. Next: FINRA, DOJ, state AGs, civil litigation.

The model that masters legal prediction will have trained on datasets like this one.

Data Integrity

No contamination. Every evaluated case is resolved after the complaint was filed—no model could have seen the outcome in training data.

Consistent prompting. All models receive identical prompts and case summaries.

Reproducible results. The full scoring code, evaluation prompts, and dataset are open source on GitHub.

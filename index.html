Methodology

How LexArena is built and why these predictions matter.

üí° Motivation

Traditional legal datasets test models on historical cases, which modern LLMs can memorize. LexArena evaluates on resolved but unseen outcomes‚Äîensuring clean testing of an AI system's legal reasoning and predictive depth.

Every forecast is made on complaint text only. Models never see the resolution, eliminating data contamination and retrospective rationalization.

üèóÔ∏è Benchmark Design

1. Case Ingestion ‚Äî We scrape SEC Litigation Releases and extract complaint PDFs.

2. Synopsis Generation ‚Äî GPT-4o creates plain-English summaries from complaint text.

3. Blind Prediction ‚Äî Models receive only the synopsis and must forecast 6 outcomes.

4. Ground Truth ‚Äî Outcomes are extracted from final SEC release text post-resolution.

üìä Prediction Metrics

  • Resolution Type Did the case settle (defendant agrees) or go to litigation (court decides)? Exact
  • Disgorgement Amount of ill-gotten gains the defendant must return to harmed investors. ¬±10%
  • Civil Penalty Fine imposed by the SEC as punishment for securities violations. ¬±10%
  • Prejudgment Interest Interest accrued on disgorgement from violation date to judgment. ¬±10%
  • Injunction Court order preventing future violations of securities laws. Exact
  • Officer/Director Bar Ban from serving as an officer or director of a public company. Exact

üéØ Scoring

Categorical metrics (resolution, injunction, officer bar) use exact match scoring‚Äîthe model must predict the correct category.

Monetary metrics (disgorgement, penalty, interest) use a 10% tolerance band. Predictions within 10% of actual amounts count as correct, accounting for reasonable estimation variance.

Overall accuracy is the weighted average across all 6 metrics, with categorical (3) and monetary (3) equally weighted.
